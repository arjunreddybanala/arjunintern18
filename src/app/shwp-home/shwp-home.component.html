SCHOOL HEALTH AND WELLNESS PROGRAM
Overveiw about SHWP

The School Health and Wellness Programme (SHWP) is a collaborative effort between the Ministry of Health and Family Welfare and the Ministry of Education to promote the health and well-being of school children across India. Launched as an operational guideline by the Honâ€™ble Prime Minister on April 14, 2018, it paved its way for creation of detailed curriculum, training and resource material and facilitator guideline for SHWP which was jointly launched by both the ministers of Health and Education in February 2020.

This programme integrates health education into the school curriculum, encouraging lifelong healthy habits. Under the initiative, two selected teachers from each school are trained as Health and Wellness Ambassadors (HWAs) to lead interactive, activity-based sessions on 11 key health themes, which are Growing up healthy, Emotional wellbeing and mental health, Interpersonal relationships, Values and responsible citizenship, Gender equality, Nutrition, health and sanitation, Promotion of healthy lifestyles, Prevention and management of substance misuse, Reproductive health and HIV prevention, Safety and security against violence and injuries and Promotion of safe use of internet, media and social media. They are assisted by student leaders, known as Health and Wellness Messengers (HWMs), who help reinforce health messages among their peers, family and community.

Implemented in government and government-aided schools, the programme emphasizes student engagement through dynamic learning approaches. To ensure effective execution, a comprehensive Management Information System (MIS) has been developed for efficient reporting and monitoring.
